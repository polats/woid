# Fallout Shelter-likes & the Severance pitch

Research pass on the room/dweller management subgenre and how it could be re-skinned around the Apple TV+ show *Severance* (sterile corporate underground, innies vs outies, MDR data refinement, perks, waffle parties, Lumon lore).

Bias: 2024–2026 sources. Captured May 2026.

---

## 1. Recent / successful Fallout Shelter-likes (2023–2026)

### Core anchor: Fallout Shelter itself

- **Fallout Shelter** (Bethesda, 2015 → still updated). Free-to-play, mobile + PC + console + Switch. **230M downloads** by its June 2025 10-year anniversary. Steam concurrent baseline ~1.6k–3.2k with a recent peak of **8,396 in April 2024** driven by the Fallout TV show ([Bethesda 10-year post](https://bethesda.net/en/article/6zT9anWe9yLVqi1F6gGPms/fallout-shelter-10th-anniversary), [HotHardware](https://hothardware.com/news/ten-years-of-fallout-shelters-huge-popularity-and-230-million-downloads), [SteamCharts](https://steamcharts.com/app/588430)).
- **What's new vs. 2015**: Quests, wasteland exploration, combat, themed seasonal events, and Amazon-Fallout-show tie-in dwellers ([GameRant retrospective](https://gamerant.com/fallout-shelter-10-year-anniversary-explained-good-why/)). Modding scene is small (no official Workshop), but PC save editors and reskins persist.

### Direct genre descendants

- **Sheltered 2** (Unicube/Team17, 2021). Cautionary tale. Devs publicly admitted it should have been Early Access; team was let go ~12 months post-launch. Mixed Steam reviews; bugs in core food/water loops, "cognitive nightmare" UI, no follow-up support ([NME review](https://www.nme.com/reviews/game-reviews/sheltered-2-review-3049654), [TurnBasedLovers](https://turnbasedlovers.com/review/sheltered-2-tbl/)). Pitch: family bunker survival with permadeath and faction politics. **Lesson**: depth without onboarding kills the genre.
- **Sheltered (1)** (2016). Still the best one-time-purchase Fallout-Shelter-like for a small family unit ([gamesradar list](https://www.gamesradar.com/games-like-fallout-shelter/)).
- **Bunker Builder Simulator** (Games Incubator, 2025). First-person construction sim where you're the *contractor* building bunkers for clients, not the overseer. Niche, mixed reception, mostly PC ([Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/1868210/Bunker_Builder_Simulator/), [Metacritic](https://www.metacritic.com/game/bunker-builder-simulator/)). Useful as a tonal contrast — diegetic blueprints and inspections.
- **Atomic Society**. Studio shut down in 2024; delisted from Steam ([PCGamesN](https://www.pcgamesn.com/fallout/atomic-society)). Drop from comp set.

### Adjacent room/colony sims (2024–2026 highlights)

- **Oxygen Not Included** (Klei, 2019 → live). The genre's gold standard for *flowing* networks. **Spaced Out!** is "Very Positive" (~80%, ~1,190 reviews). Klei shipped three paid DLCs in 14 months: **Frosty Planet (Jul 2024)**, **Bionic Booster (Dec 2024)**, **Prehistoric Planet (Jun 2025)**, plus a Nov 2025 free QoL pass and a Mar 2026 Unity-engine bump ([Klei forums](https://forums.kleientertainment.com/forums/forum/251-oxygen-not-included-spaced-out-general-discussion/), [SteamDB Nov 2025 patch](https://steamdb.info/patchnotes/20870007/), [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxygen_Not_Included)). Multi-asteroid management is the live-service direction.
- **Two Point Museum** (Two Point/Sega, **Mar 2025**). Critical hit; *Inverse* called it one of the best strategy games of 2025. Major new system: **expeditions** — dispatch staff off-map to fetch exhibits, each subcategory has bespoke mechanics; partition-walls replace fixed rooms ([PC Gamer review](https://www.pcgamer.com/games/sim/two-point-museum-review/), [TechRadar](https://www.techradar.com/gaming/two-point-museum-review), [Inverse](https://www.inverse.com/gaming/two-points-museum-review)).
- **Two Point Hospital / Campus**. Still the canonical "wholesome corporate" management aesthetic — directly relevant to a Lumon pastiche.
- **Evil Genius 2: World Domination** (Rebellion, 2021). Multi-floor underground lair, minion specialization, world-map global ops, sets and traps. Praised base-builder; criticized for slow inter-floor movement (no elevators) ([DSOGaming](https://www.dsogaming.com/special/reviews/evil-genius-2-pc-review/), [GameRant guide](https://gamerant.com/evil-genius-2-best-base-layout-build-guide/)). **Closest existing tonal match for a sterile-corporate-underground vibe.**
- **Dome Keeper** (Bippinbits, 2022, active updates through 2024). Tower-defense without towers + roguelike runs + vertical mining. "Forgotten roguelike masterpiece" ([Indiecator 2024](https://indiecator.org/2024/11/29/indietail-dome-keeper/), [Metacritic](https://www.metacritic.com/game/dome-keeper/)). Showed how to fuse run-based meta with base-tend.
- **Going Medieval** (Foxy Voxel). Active in 2025 (patch 0.27.07, Oct 2025), 80% recent positive ([SteamDB patch](https://steamdb.info/patchnotes/20240608/)). Voxel multi-floor building; relevant for vertical construction.
- **Cosmoteer** (Walternate, EA). 0.28.1 (Jan 2025), 0.30.0a in 2025 ([Cosmoteer wiki](https://cosmoteer.wiki.gg/wiki/Version_History)). Free-placement modular ship-as-base — the anti-grid model.
- **Stranded: Alien Dawn** (Haemimont, 2023, DLC through 2024). "Strong" critic average. Shallower than RimWorld but cleaner UI ([Metacritic](https://www.metacritic.com/game/stranded-alien-dawn/)).
- **War Hospital** (Brave Lamb/Nacon, **Jan 2024**). WW1 field-hospital management. Mixed (~57% Steam), critic average 63 — gameplay solid but UI and tooltip gaps. Frostpunk-adjacent, not Two-Point-adjacent ([Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/1553000/War_Hospital/), [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/War_Hospital)). Tonal cue for grim medical management.
- **Mewgenics** (McMillen, **EA 2025 → full 2026**). OpenCritic 89, "Must-Play". Not a base sim, but the **breeding-as-progression** loop and procedural-trait dwellers are very portable to a Severance-style "innie genealogy" system ([GameSpot review](https://www.gamespot.com/reviews/mewgenics-review-a-near-purrfect-roguelite-adventure/1900-6418456/), [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mewgenics)).
- **RimWorld**, **Dwarf Fortress** (2022 Steam release, ongoing). Comparison anchors only — both are deeper colony sims with emergent narrative engines that any modern Shelter-like is measured against.

### Drop from comp set

- **Spirittea** — life sim, not management.
- **Lessaria / Diplomacy Is Not An Option** — tower-defense city builders, only loosely adjacent.
- **Endzone 2** (2025 EA) — surface post-apoc colony, not bunker.

### Severance fan/jam space

Strongly active and a useful audience signal:

- **Macrodata Refinement** by devwithlex — viral browser MDR sim covered by GameSpot and Gizmodo ([GameSpot](https://www.gamespot.com/articles/free-severance-browser-game-lets-you-refine-macrodata-like-a-pro/1100-6529773/), [Gizmodo](https://gizmodo.com/this-severance-game-is-damaging-my-real-job-writing-about-severance-2000569367)).
- **MDR Terminal: The Refiners App** (jorge/risostudio).
- **Lumon-8 (Pico1k)** — 1KB demake of MDR ([itch](https://liquidream.itch.io/lumon8-1k)).
- **andrewchilicki/LumonMDR** — open-source web MDR clone on GitHub.
- The itch [`severance` tag](https://itch.io/games/tag-severance) and [`mdr` tag](https://itch.io/games/tag-mdr) have growing entries since season 2 aired.

**Implication**: there is *strong demonstrated demand* for Severance-themed micro-experiences, but **no one has shipped a full management sim in this skin yet**. That's the gap.

### Mobile / APAC note

No notable Korean/Chinese vault-management hit surfaced in 2024–25; APAC mobile-strategy momentum is in idle-RPG (Legend of Mushroom, ~$100M Jan–Apr 2024) and 4X, not Shelter-clones ([Sensor Tower 2024 APAC](https://sensortower.com/blog/state-of-mobile-games-in-apac-2024-report)). Genre slot is uncrowded outside Bethesda.

## 2. New mechanics introduced since Fallout Shelter (2015)

### Construction & space
- **Free placement vs grid rooms** — Cosmoteer, Going Medieval, Two Point Museum's partition walls (a 2025 step away from rigid pre-set rooms) ([PC Gamer](https://www.pcgamer.com/games/sim/two-point-museum-review/)).
- **True multi-floor with vertical traversal cost** — Evil Genius 2 (40-second stair traversals as a real cost; relevant to "elevator down to the severed floor").
- **Vertical *exploration*, not just construction** — Dome Keeper digs down for resources between waves.

### Resource / network systems
- **Pipes, wires, gases, heat as physical fluids** — ONI is the canonical reference; nobody since has matched its sim depth.
- **Production chains with intermediate goods** — ONI, Two Point, Evil Genius 2's loot-laundering chain (steal → fence → cash).
- **Per-service / per-room mood requirements** — Two Point series' "buzz" and decoration scoring.

### Dwellers as people
- **Moodlets, breakdowns, relationships, romances** — RimWorld popularized; Dwarf Fortress deepened; Stranded: Alien Dawn streamlined.
- **Procedural traits / genetics as progression** — Mewgenics is the freshest example (collars + gene combos); RimWorld's gene system (Biotech DLC).
- **Skill trees vs S.P.E.C.I.A.L.** — Sheltered/Sheltered 2 used skill XP; Two Point Campus uses staff training rooms as a literal building type.

### Pacing & structure
- **Scenario timers / wave pressure** — Dome Keeper raids; This War of Mine's day/night split; Frostpunk's storm.
- **Roguelike meta-progression on top of base-building** — Dome Keeper is the cleanest example.
- **Narrative event cards** — Reigns lineage; Crusader Kings; Stranded: Alien Dawn dispatches; RimWorld's "storyteller AI."
- **Above-ground excursions / dispatch missions** — Two Point Museum's expeditions (2025), Evil Genius 2's world map, Fallout Shelter's wasteland (2016 update).

### Live-service & community
- **Seasonal content drops** — Fallout Shelter's TV-tie-in dwellers; ONI's three paid packs in 14 months.
- **Workshop / mods** — RimWorld and Cosmoteer dominate; ONI's mod scene is large but unofficial.
- **Idle / offline progress / prestige** — Mobile-only patterns; Fallout Shelter has a soft version (ongoing production while closed).

### Presentation
- **Diegetic / found-footage UI** — niche but powerful (Lucas Pope's lineage; surveillance-cam framing in horror sims). **Largely unexplored in this subgenre** and a strong angle for Severance.
- **Cross-section "ant farm" view** — Fallout Shelter still owns this silhouette. Project Highrise and Tiny Tower are the cousins.

## 3. Critical consensus on Fallout Shelter

### Why it stayed successful for 10 years
- **Free-to-play with non-coercive monetization** — lunchboxes are nice-to-have, not required ([GameRant](https://gamerant.com/fallout-shelter-10-year-anniversary-explained-good-why/)).
- **Cross-platform sticky install base** — iOS, Android, PC, Xbox, PS, Switch.
- **Brand carry** from Fallout 4 launch + Amazon TV show resurgence (2024 Steam CCU spike to 8,396).
- **The cross-section silhouette** is iconic and instantly readable.
- **Bethesda kept content drips going** for a decade (quests 2016, TV dwellers 2024).

### Long-running complaints
- **Rush RNG feels rigged** — recurring Steam threads claim displayed % doesn't match outcome ([Steam discussion](https://steamcommunity.com/app/588430/discussions/0/2741975115081838531/)).
- **Dweller homogeneity** — S.P.E.C.I.A.L. + outfit, no real personality, no relationships beyond breeding ([TechRaptor "18 Things"](https://techraptor.net/gaming/opinion/18-things-fallout-shelter-needs-fix)).
- **Late-game wall** — once vault is stable, only thing left is grind for legendaries; survival mode swings from trivial to brutal at population thresholds.
- **Caps economy is glacial** without lunchboxes.
- **No deep mod/UGC pipeline** caps creative re-engagement.

### Why clones failed
- **Sheltered 2**: shipped as 1.0 what should have been EA, overstacked systems with no onboarding, studio collapsed within a year ([NME](https://www.nme.com/reviews/game-reviews/sheltered-2-review-3049654), [TurnBasedLovers](https://turnbasedlovers.com/review/sheltered-2-tbl/)).
- **Atomic Society**: thin content, studio shut down 2024.
- **Bunker Builder Simulator**: niche framing (you're the contractor) doesn't deliver the *overseer fantasy*.

The gap nobody filled: a **modern Fallout Shelter with character depth, real production chains, and a strong narrative skin**. Two Point series owns the "cute corporate" tone but isn't underground/dark; Evil Genius owns the underground lair but is comedic Bond pastiche; ONI owns the systems but is alien.

## 4. Severance-flavored design hooks

Eight mechanic-to-theme matches, one line each.

1. **Severed-floor cross-section view + walled-off "Outie" surface scenes.** Map Fallout Shelter's iconic silhouette to *the severed floor*; gate the surface as separate, mostly UI-only "outie" interludes — the player literally never sees the surface during refinement gameplay.
2. **Dwellers as innies with separate outie shadows** (Mewgenics-style trait pairing; the show's innie/outie split). One body, two stat sheets, two relationship graphs; dying as an innie ≠ dying as a person, and the moral weight of that is the entire emotional engine.
3. **MDR as a production chain** (ONI/Two Point production chains; existing fan MDR sims). Refining "scary numbers" into the five bins is a literal intermediate-goods loop — bins fill quotas, quotas unlock perks, perks unlock new wings (Music & Dance Experience, Wellness, Break Room, Testing Floor).
4. **Perks as live-service seasonal drops** (Fallout Shelter's TV-tie-in cadence; ONI's DLC packs). Waffle Party, Melon Bar, Egg Bar, Music Dance Experience — each is a one-shot scripted *event* that mutates the base for a session, like a Two Point challenge card.
5. **Narrative event cards delivered by Mr. Milchick / Ms. Cobel** (Reigns / Crusader Kings event lineage). Diegetic management interruptions: handbook revisions, surprise inspections, kindness reforms, finger-traps as morale items.
6. **The Break Room as a moodlet/punishment system** (RimWorld moodlets; Sheltered's mental-break loop). Failing innies get sent to the Break Room — a distinct minigame screen that drains a "compliance" stat over time. Real consequence, not just a debuff.
7. **Diegetic surveillance-cam UI / found-footage framing** (largely unexplored; nearest is Pope-likes). The overseer view *is* Lumon's CCTV — the player is implicated, fish-eye lens, fluorescent buzz, no fourth-wall HUD. This is the visual hook nobody else in the subgenre owns.
8. **Cold Harbor as a roguelike meta-run / endgame** (Dome Keeper meta-progression). Each "file" Mark refines is a roguelike run; completing Cold Harbor is the meta-prestige that unlocks new innie consciousnesses. Permits meaningful loop without requiring infinite content.

Bonus theme hooks worth flagging:
- **Goats program** as a parallel sub-base with completely different rules (analogous to ONI's secondary planetoids).
- **Testing Floor** as a locked, mysterious second basement layer — a "bottom of the dome" the player can't enter for hours of play, then can.
- **The Handbook** as the literal tutorial/lore document; revisable in-game by Milchick à la Frostpunk laws.
